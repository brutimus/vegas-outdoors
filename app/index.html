Vegas Outdoors

Las Vegas – a hidden gem of outdoor activities in the Mojave Desert. While mostly written off as only a city of glitz and glamour, Las Vegas has a lot more to offer both the local and the tourist.

Whether you're looking for the best bicycle commuting routes around the city or the best resources for scaling that 20-pitch route in Red Rocks, we'll point you in the right direction and help you get out there and do it!

via Bicycle

Commute to work in the morning, do a quick 40-miler after work, then head out to Cottonwood to get dusty on the weekend. No matter your style of cycling, we'll give you the lowdown on how to find it.

Commuting

The Las Vegas valley may not be at the top of your list when thinking of bike commuter friendly cities, but we hold our own! Most routes within the neighborhoods are safe and navigable, but you may still find a few hiccups when riding a long haul across the city.

Road

Sunny blue skies. Smooth roads. Incredible scenery. The Las Vegas area is a great place to get out on your road bike and put in some serious miles. Whether you're shooting for distance or tough hill climbing, it's all within reach! With so few bad weather days, you can get out there nearly any day of the year.

Mountain

Take the family out for an easy stroll on some of the milder trails at Cottonwood or gather your toughest friends and see who survives Bootleg Canyon. The Las Vegas area nearly has it all when it comes to mountain biking!

Hiking

If it's hiking you're looking for, chances are you'll end up in either Red Rocks or Mt. Charleston, depending on the season. Hit the classics – like Calico Tanks – in Red Rocks in the cooler months. Then, head up to Mt. Charleston to backpack the summit loop over a day or two when the valley is heating up in the summer (or do this in the winter, too!) But that's not all! Check out Anniversary Narrows and Arizona Hot Springs while you're at it.

View details »

Climbing

You needn't look far to see why both ameture and professional rock climbers swarm to Red Rocks during the shoulder seasons. Head out with a group for craggin' at countless walls or grab a buddy and sack up for some of the world-famous trad routes reaching 20+ pitches.

View details »

Canyoneering

Las Vegas may not compare to the likes of Zion or Canyonlands when it comes to technical canyoneering, but we have enough to hold our own for the serious canyoneer. Most canyons in Red Rocks - reaching upwards of 12 hours in length - are not for the beginner. If you're looking for something a little more tame, take a look at Mt. Charleston, Keyhole Canyon , Frenchman Mountain or the Jean area. If you can't find something suitable here, Utah's parks are only a hop away!
