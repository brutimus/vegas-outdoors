Start

You've finally decided to give bicycle commuting a go! Here are a handful of tips to get you out there and keep you safe.

  • Get your bike into good repair: Oil that chain with some quality chain lube. You'd be surprised at the difference this makes!
  • Plan your route: Use our routes below or scan Google Maps Bicycle layer to find a safe route. Fancy a weekend test ride to work out the kinks?
  • Dress appropriately: No, not spandex – that's rarely appropriate! This is an obvious tip, dress cool for the summer, warm for the winter. Chilly air is surprisingly good and penetrating nearly every type of clothing, so take gloves in the winter. Maybe throw in an extra undershirt in the summer, just in case.
  • Be seen: Want another reason to curse the DST transition in November? You'll be riding home in the dark. Get yourself some blinkies – front and rear! – and maybe a reflective vest.
  • Keep your ride safe: Unfortunately, people love stealing bikes. If you'll be leaving your bike outside, get a hefty bike lock. The big U Locks are commonly known as your safest bet.

Routes

Bicycle commuting in Las Vegas is rarely straight forward. With no established bicycle routes through the city, daily commutes become a guessing game of where bike trails and lanes connect. Establishing a map of preferred routes will concentrate bicycle traffic on fewer streets to increase safety and allow more civil resources to be used on these travel corridors.
